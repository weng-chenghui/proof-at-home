Three ways to prove, one standard of credit

Pick the path that fits your skills and tools. Every path produces the same permanent credit.

✍️

By Hand

Write a formal proof in Lean or Rocq yourself. No API key, no budget, no AI — just your knowledge and a compiler.

pah proof submit prob_001 proof.lean
👥

Pair with AI

Export a conjecture and solve it interactively with your AI coding assistant — Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, or Windsurf. No API key needed.

pah conjecture export prob_001 # ... prove with your assistant ... pah proof submit prob_001 proof.lean \ --method pair-proved
⚡

Fully Automated

Set a budget and let the CLI drive an LLM to generate and verify proofs automatically. You donate compute; the system does the rest.

pah contribution run
↓ ↓ ↓

Compiler verifies. You get credited.

Every proof is checked by the Lean or Rocq compiler. Then it's sealed with a permanent NFT — a verifiable record of who contributed, how, and when. Same credit. Same NFT. Your method is recorded, your contribution is equal.

What the NFT means

The NFT is not about ownership — proofs are public domain (CC0). The NFT is a permanent receipt: "This theorem was formalized because of your work."

Think of it like a donor's name on a university building. The building is public. The name records who made it possible. Your NFT records your contribution, your proof method, and a cryptographic signature — pinned to IPFS, mintable on-chain.

More than proving

Proving is just one way to contribute. You can also certify others' proofs, author new conjectures, write expositions that explain proofs, or collaborate by improving existing work. Every role earns its own NFT credit.
